The global IT services market is growing!

The global market for IT services is valued at $716 billion, with more than three-quarters (about $556 billion) covered by outsourcers. According to forecasts, the IT outsourcing market will reach $806.63 billion over the next three years.

What is the most widely used tech stack now?

The top preferred programming languages by modern specialists are:

  • JavaScript - 67.9%

  • HTML/CSS - 54.93%

  • SQL - 52.64%

Python and TypeScript are lagging at 40%. Ruby is staying at the bottom of the list at 6.72%.


IT Outsourcing Market: Who is wanted?

Regarding the cloud platform employment statistics, 55.17% of software engineers work with Amazon Web Services. 30.36% prefer Microsoft Azure, and 26.1% go for Google Cloud. Oracle Cloud Infrastructure, IBM Cloud, and Colocation close the list at 1%.

The fastest growing in-demand job skills among software engineers are:

  • quantum computing (135% growth over 5 years)

  • connected technologies (104%)

  • FinTech (96%)

  • AI and Machine Learning (71%)

  • cloud tech (28%)

  • parallel computing (17%)

Top onsumers of Software Development Outsourcing Services

Outsourcing involvement is most demanded and frequently employed in the financial services sector (where 72% of companies go for outsourcing), retail/wholesale market (60%), and manufacturing (59%), with specialized tech services, government/nonprofit, and healthcare not lagging far behind.


How much does IT outsourcing cost in 2022?

The European IT market is dominated by Germany, the UK, and France. While the global IT arena is run by the US and China, all of which are the leading providers of reliable outsourcing opportunities. Countries like Poland, Ukraine, and Belgium are moving up the ladder as popular alternative destinations.

Global rates of outsourcing providers vary dramatically. Africa and South Asia are among the cheapest options ($15-25 per hour). Central and Eastern European services cost a bit more ($30-50 per hour) yet granting more reliability. Meanwhile, Western European, Australian, and North American outsourcers are asking for up to $200 per hour.
